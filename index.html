DARJYO - Project Q29 in South Africa


Our intent is to create a network of sensors scattered across our fields, recording data about soil moisture, temperature, and even pest activity to a central hub.
It's real-time data that lets us tailor our farming practices with laser focus.

To conserve water: The system flags areas that are good for drip irrigation, saving our water resource for when our crops truly need it.

Surprise frost: Predictive analytics will warn us beforehand, so we can cover our crops or set off sprinklers.

DARJYO implementing Climate-smart agriculture is about rediscovering the wisdom of our ancestors – with a tech twist.
We're talking about practices like cover cropping.

Hay bales suppress weeds, retain moisture, and even add nutrients to the soil – a natural fertilizer factory right there in our field!

And with drone technology, we can even seed these cover crops with pinpoint accuracy.

There are challenges, like making sure tech is accessible and ensuring the data is secure.
DARJYO is creating a digital symphony for our farm.
Data plays the melody, guiding our decisions, while traditional practices like cover cropping harmonize the whole thing.

The result? A healthier planet, thriving crops, and maybe even a reduction in greenhouse gas emissions.
